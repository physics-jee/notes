Personal Notes on Structure, Meaning and Mathematical Rigor

About These Notes

Students often ask: "Is NCERT sufficient for JEE Advanced?" But they rarely ask a more fundamental question: What is the difference between knowledge and understanding?

Procuring knowledge is largely a sensory input process.You read something. You listen to something. Information enters through the senses.

Understanding is a separate mental process. It requires deliberate internal work. Often, knowledge acquisition and understanding happen simultaneously, so most people fail to distinguish between the two. But those who ruminate and introspect more begin to see the difference clearly.

Understanding something means studying it structurally. Suppose a table is kept in front of you. First, you observe and describe it: it has a horizontal surface, it stands on legs, it is solid, it has a particular color and material. These are its properties.

Then you examine your relation to it. You use the table to place objects at a convenient height while working. Different entities may have different relations with the same table.

Now consider change. Suppose the table changes color from white to grey to black and back again. A specific configuration is a state. When properties change with time, we study dynamics. An event is a transition between states, often described in terms of cause and effect.

To study the table means organizing: its properties, its relations, its states, and the events that change those states. This internal organization is understanding.

NCERT is a source of knowledge. Any book is a source of knowledge. No knowledge source is sufficient by itself. Understanding must be constructed independently.

These notes are not presented as the final or the one only correct way to understand physics. They represent my way of organizing and thinking about the subject. I may be right in some places and wrong in others. That is acceptable. The purpose is not authority, but clarity.
