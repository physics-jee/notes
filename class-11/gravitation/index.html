Gravitation

Universal force governing motion of celestial and terrestrial bodies.

1. Universal Law of Gravitation

Every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

\[ F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \]
  • Always attractive
  • Long-range force
Gravitation is weak but universal — it never switches off.

2. Gravitational Field & Intensity

The gravitational field at a point is the force experienced per unit test mass placed at that point.

\[ \vec g = \frac{\vec F}{m} \quad \Rightarrow \quad g = \frac{GM}{r^2} \]
Field description avoids test-mass dependence.

3. Gravitational Potential & Potential Energy

Gravitational potential is the work done per unit mass in bringing a body from infinity to a point.

\[ V = -\frac{GM}{r} \]
\[ U = -\frac{GMm}{r} \]
Negative potential energy indicates a bound system.

4. Relation Between Field and Potential

\[ \vec g = -\nabla V \quad (\text{radial: } g = -\frac{dV}{dr}) \]
Force is the slope of potential.

5. Motion of Planets & Satellites

Circular orbital motion arises naturally from gravitational attraction.

Orbital speed: \[ v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \]
Time period: \[ T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{r^3}{GM}} \]
Gravity supplies centripetal force.

6. Kepler’s Laws (Consequences of Newton’s Law)

  • Law of orbits — elliptical paths
  • Law of areas — constant areal velocity
  • Law of periods — \(T^2 \propto r^3\)
Kepler’s laws are empirical; Newton explains them.

7. Escape Velocity

Escape velocity is the minimum speed required to escape a planet’s gravitational influence.

\[ v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} \]
Escape velocity depends on planet, not on mass of projectile.

8. Earth Satellites

  • Geostationary satellites
  • Polar satellites
Geostationary radius: \[ r = \left(\frac{GM T^2}{4\pi^2}\right)^{1/3} \]

9. Weightlessness

Weightlessness occurs when normal reaction becomes zero, not when gravity disappears.

Orbiting astronauts are continuously falling.

10. Common Mistakes

  • Confusing field with force
  • Assuming zero gravity in orbit
  • Forgetting negative sign in potential energy
