Elasticity

Conceptual Overview

Elasticity is the property of a body to regain its original shape and size after the deforming force is removed. It is studied via Stress-Strain relationships.

Core Formulas

\[ \text{Stress} = \frac{F}{A}, \quad \text{Strain} = \frac{\Delta L}{L}, \quad Y = \frac{\text{Stress}}{\text{Strain}} \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Hooke's Law: Stress is proportional to strain within the elastic limit.
  • Moduli of Elasticity: Young's (length), Bulk (volume), and Rigidity (shape).
  • Elastic potential energy stored in a stretched wire.
