Gaseous State

Macroscopic description of gases under mechanical constraints.

1. State Variables

The thermodynamic state of a gas is described using measurable macroscopic quantities.

  • Pressure (P) — mechanical constraint via container walls
  • Volume (V) — geometrical constraint of the container
  • Temperature (T) — thermal state of the gas
  • Amount of gas (n) — number of moles
In gases, pressure and volume are not intrinsic — they are imposed by boundaries.

2. Pressure of a Gas

Gas pressure arises due to collisions of gas molecules with the container walls.

\[ P = \frac{F}{A} \]
  • Scalar quantity
  • Acts normal to surfaces

3. Boyle’s Law (Isothermal Process)

For a fixed mass of gas at constant temperature, pressure is inversely proportional to volume.

\[ P \propto \frac{1}{V} \quad \Rightarrow \quad PV = \text{constant} \]
Boyle’s law reflects a purely mechanical constraint at constant thermal state.

4. Charles’ Law (Isobaric Process)

At constant pressure, the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature.

\[ V \propto T \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{V}{T} = \text{constant} \]
Temperature must be measured on the Kelvin scale.

5. Gay–Lussac’s Law (Isochoric Process)

At constant volume, pressure of a gas is directly proportional to absolute temperature.

\[ P \propto T \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{P}{T} = \text{constant} \]

6. Combined Gas Law

For a fixed mass of gas:

\[ \frac{PV}{T} = \text{constant} \]
Gas laws connect different mechanical constraints through temperature.

7. Ideal Gas Equation

Combining all gas laws gives the equation of state for an ideal gas.

\[ PV = nRT \]
  • \(n\): number of moles
  • \(R\): universal gas constant
The ideal gas is a model — real gases approximate it at low pressure and high temperature.

8. Density of a Gas

\[ \rho = \frac{PM}{RT} \]
  • \(M\): molar mass
  • Explains why lighter gases rise

9. Limitations of Gas Laws

  • Break down at high pressure
  • Fail at very low temperatures
  • Ignore intermolecular forces and molecular volume
Limitations motivate the kinetic theory and real gas models.

10. Common Mistakes

  • Using Celsius instead of Kelvin
  • Applying gas laws to changing mass
  • Forgetting process constraints (iso-*)
