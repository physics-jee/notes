Physical World

Foundational assumptions behind all of physics.

Why This Chapter Comes First

Before solving problems, we must clarify what we are solving. Physics does not begin with equations. It begins with identifying what exists, what can be measured, and how knowledge about it is organized.

This chapter is not computational. It is structural. It defines the framework within which all later mathematics operates.

Working Definitions

Physical World: The objective reality that exists independent of individual perception.

Physics: The quantitative study of matter and energy as they exist and change in space and time.

How This Chapter Should Be Read

This chapter does not introduce formulas. It introduces assumptions. Many of these assumptions are so fundamental that textbooks rarely state them explicitly.

Here, we make them explicit. Because solving problems requires clarity about the structure of the world we are modeling.

Scope of This Chapter

This chapter clarifies:

  • What we mean by the physical world
  • What physics studies
  • How scientific knowledge is structured
  • Why measurement and units come first
