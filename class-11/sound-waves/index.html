Sound Waves

Longitudinal waves in elastic media.

1. Nature of Sound Waves

Sound waves are longitudinal mechanical waves that propagate through a material medium.

  • Require a medium
  • Cannot propagate in vacuum

2. Compressional Waves

  • Compressions → high pressure regions
  • Rarefactions → low pressure regions
Sound propagation is pressure oscillation, not particle transport.

3. Speed of Sound

\[ v = \sqrt{\frac{E}{\rho}} \]
  • Solids: Young’s modulus
  • Liquids: Bulk modulus
  • Gases: Adiabatic bulk modulus

4. Speed of Sound in Gases

\[ v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M}} \]
Speed of sound in gases depends on temperature, not pressure.

5. Intensity & Loudness

\[ I = \frac{P}{A} \]
  • Intensity ∝ amplitude²
  • Loudness is subjective

6. Doppler Effect

\[ f' = f \frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s} \]
  • Observed frequency differs due to relative motion
Doppler effect arises from changing wavefront spacing.

7. Standing Sound Waves

  • Open pipes → antinode at ends
  • Closed pipes → node at closed end
Open pipe: \[ f_n = \frac{nv}{2L} \]
Closed pipe: \[ f_n = \frac{nv}{4L} \quad (n = 1,3,5\dots) \]
