Rotational Mechanics

Conceptual Overview

Rotational motion involves the rotation of a rigid body about a fixed axis. It is the rotational analog of linear motion, with Torque and Angular Momentum playing critical roles.

Core Formulas

\[ \tau = I\alpha, \quad L = I\omega, \quad K.E_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2, \quad I = \sum m_i r_i^2 \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Moment of Inertia depends on mass distribution and axis of rotation.
  • Parallel and Perpendicular axes theorems for calculated Moments of Inertia.
  • Conservation of Angular Momentum when net external torque is zero.
  • Rolling motion: Combination of Translation and Rotation.
