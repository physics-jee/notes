Motion in One Dimension

The first complete mathematical treatment of change.

Why This Chapter Matters

This is the first chapter where physics becomes fully operational. Earlier chapters clarified structure and measurement. Here, we begin applying mathematics to describe motion precisely.

We now define position as a function of time, and from it derive velocity and acceleration. Motion is no longer described qualitatively — it is expressed through measurable relations.

What This Chapter Does

  • Defines motion relative to a frame of reference
  • Introduces displacement as a signed quantity
  • Defines velocity and acceleration rigorously
  • Connects algebraic equations with geometric graphs
  • Builds the standard equations of uniformly accelerated motion

Conceptual Position

This chapter belongs to kinematics. Kinematics describes how motion occurs, without yet discussing forces.

We are studying change itself — not the cause of change.

How It Should Be Studied

  • Be strict with sign conventions
  • Interpret graphs geometrically
  • Understand derivations — avoid memorization
  • Relate equations to physical meaning
