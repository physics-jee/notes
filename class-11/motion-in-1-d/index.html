Motion in One Dimension

The first quantitative description of motion.

Chapter Overview

Motion in one dimension is the first formal step in mechanics. In this chapter, we learn how to describe motion using space and time, without worrying about the causes of motion.

This chapter belongs to kinematics — the branch of mechanics concerned only with describing motion, not explaining it.

Kinematics answers how objects move, not why they move.

How to Study This Chapter

  • Focus on definitions and sign conventions
  • Understand graphs geometrically
  • Do not memorize equations without derivation
