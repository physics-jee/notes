Frame of Reference

Motion cannot be described without specifying an observer. The coordinate system attached to an observer is called a frame of reference.

A frame of reference consists of:

  • A reference point (origin)
  • A direction (axis)
  • A clock to measure time
Motion is not absolute — it is always measured relative to a frame of reference.

Relativity of Rest and Motion

An object at rest in one frame may be in motion in another. For example, a passenger sitting in a moving train is:

  • At rest relative to the train
  • In motion relative to the ground

Inertial Frames (Preview)

A frame of reference in which Newton’s laws hold true is called an inertial frame. In this chapter, we assume all frames are inertial.
