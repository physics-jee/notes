Work, Energy & Power

A scalar approach to understanding motion and change.

1. Work

Work is said to be done when a force produces displacement in its direction.

\[ W = \vec F \cdot \vec s = Fs\cos\theta \]
  • Work depends on force, displacement, and angle between them
  • Zero work if displacement is perpendicular to force
Work is path-dependent for variable forces.

2. Work by a Variable Force

When force varies with position, work is defined via integration.

\[ W = \int \vec F \cdot d\vec r \]

Graphically, work equals the area under the force–displacement curve.

3. Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy possessed by a body due to its motion.

\[ K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \]
Kinetic energy depends on the frame of reference.

4. Work–Energy Theorem

The net work done on a body equals the change in its kinetic energy.

\[ W_{\text{net}} = \Delta K \]

This theorem is valid even when forces are variable.

Work–energy theorem is Newton’s second law in scalar form.

5. Potential Energy

Potential energy is energy associated with position or configuration.

Gravitational Potential Energy (Near Earth)

\[ U = mgh \]

Spring Potential Energy

\[ U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \]
Potential energy is defined only for conservative forces.

6. Conservative & Non-Conservative Forces

Conservative
  • Path independent work
  • Potential energy definable
  • Gravity, spring force
Non-Conservative
  • Path dependent work
  • No single potential energy
  • Friction

7. Conservation of Mechanical Energy

If only conservative forces act, total mechanical energy remains constant.

\[ K + U = \text{constant} \]
Conservation laws reduce differential equations to algebra.

8. Power

Power is the rate of doing work.

Average Power: \[ P_{\text{avg}} = \frac{W}{t} \]

Instantaneous Power: \[ P = \vec F \cdot \vec v \]
High power means fast energy transfer, not necessarily large force.

9. Common Mistakes

  • Using energy conservation when friction is present
  • Forgetting reference level in potential energy
  • Confusing work with force
