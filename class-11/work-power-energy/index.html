Work, Power, and Energy

Conceptual Overview

Concepts of work and energy are scalar quantities that provide a powerful way to solve mechanical problems using energy conservation principles.

Core Formulas

\[ W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d}, \quad K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^2, \quad P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Work-Energy Theorem: Net work equals change in Kinetic Energy.
  • Conservative forces (Path independent) vs. Non-conservative forces.
  • Potential energy: Gravitational ($mgh$) and Elastic ($\frac{1}{2}kx^2$).
  • Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy.
