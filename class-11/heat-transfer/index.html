Heat Transfer

Mechanisms by which heat flows from one body to another.

1. Modes of Heat Transfer

Heat can be transferred between systems by three distinct mechanisms.

  • Conduction — without bulk motion
  • Convection — with bulk motion of fluid
  • Radiation — via electromagnetic waves
The mechanism depends on the medium, not on how hot the body is.

2. Conduction

Conduction is heat transfer through a material without any macroscopic movement of matter.

Rate of heat flow: \[ \frac{dQ}{dt} = kA\frac{(T_1 - T_2)}{L} \]
  • \(k\): thermal conductivity
  • High for metals, low for insulators
Temperature gradient drives conduction, not temperature itself.

3. Thermal Resistance

Conduction problems are simplified using thermal resistance, analogous to electrical resistance.

\[ R = \frac{L}{kA} \]

\[ \frac{dQ}{dt} = \frac{\Delta T}{R} \]
  • Series and parallel combinations apply
  • Extremely useful for composite slabs

4. Convection

Convection is heat transfer by bulk motion of a fluid.

  • Natural convection — due to density differences
  • Forced convection — due to external agency (fan, pump)
Newton’s law of cooling: \[ \frac{dQ}{dt} = hA(T - T_0) \]
Convection requires a fluid; solids do not convect.

5. Newton’s Law of Cooling

The rate of cooling of a body is proportional to the temperature difference with surroundings for small temperature differences.

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} \propto (T - T_0) \]
  • Valid for small \(\Delta T\)
  • Depends on surface conditions

6. Radiation

Radiation is heat transfer via electromagnetic waves and does not require a material medium.

Stefan–Boltzmann law: \[ P = \sigma A T^4 \]
  • Occurs even in vacuum
  • Depends strongly on temperature
Radiation dominates at high temperatures and in vacuum.

7. Emissivity & Absorptivity

Real bodies deviate from ideal blackbody behavior.

\[ P = e\sigma A T^4 \]
  • \(e = 1\): perfect black body
  • Good absorbers are good emitters
Surface nature matters more than material bulk.

8. Comparison of Heat Transfer Mechanisms

Conduction
  • Requires medium
  • No bulk motion
Radiation
  • No medium required
  • Fast at high temperature

9. Common Mistakes

  • Using Newton’s law for large temperature differences
  • Ignoring surface area in radiation problems
  • Confusing conduction with convection
