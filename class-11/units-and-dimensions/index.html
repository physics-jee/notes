Units & Dimensions

The formal structure that makes physical equations meaningful.

Chapter Overview

Physics expresses natural phenomena quantitatively. Every measurable statement must be written as a numerical value multiplied by a unit.

This chapter establishes the formal system that ensures such expressions are consistent, universal, and logically valid.

Units give physical meaning to numbers. Dimensions ensure equations are structurally consistent.

Without this framework, mathematical expressions would be physically meaningless.

What This Chapter Does

  • Defines physical quantities rigorously
  • Introduces systems of units (SI system)
  • Distinguishes fundamental and derived quantities
  • Explains dimensions and dimensional formulae
  • Establishes the principle of dimensional homogeneity
  • Clarifies the power and limits of dimensional analysis
