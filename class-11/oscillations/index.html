Oscillations

Motion that repeats itself in time.

1. Oscillatory Motion

Oscillatory motion is a periodic to-and-fro motion of a body about a fixed equilibrium position.

  • Motion repeats in time
  • Occurs about a stable equilibrium
Not all periodic motions are oscillatory, but all oscillations are periodic.

2. Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

A particle executes simple harmonic motion if the restoring force acting on it is directly proportional to its displacement from equilibrium and directed toward it.

\[ F = -kx \]

Using Newton’s second law:

\[ a = -\omega^2 x \quad \text{where } \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \]
SHM is defined by the *nature of force*, not by the shape of motion.

3. Displacement, Velocity & Acceleration

Displacement: \[ x = A\sin(\omega t + \phi) \]

Velocity: \[ v = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2} \]

Acceleration: \[ a = -\omega^2 x \]
  • Velocity maximum at mean position
  • Acceleration maximum at extremes

4. Time Period & Frequency

\[ T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \quad\quad f = \frac{1}{T} \]

The time period depends only on system parameters, not on amplitude (for SHM).

Isochronism is a defining feature of SHM.

5. Energy in SHM

Kinetic Energy: \[ K = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2) \]

Potential Energy: \[ U = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x^2 \]

Total Energy: \[ E = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 A^2 \]
Energy continuously transforms between kinetic and potential forms.

6. Mass–Spring System

A block attached to an ideal spring on a frictionless surface executes SHM.

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}} \]

7. Simple Pendulum

A simple pendulum performs SHM for small angular displacements.

\[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{l}{g}} \]
  • Valid only for small angles
  • Independent of mass of bob
Pendulum SHM arises from angular approximation: \(\sin\theta \approx \theta\).

8. Common Mistakes

  • Calling all periodic motion SHM
  • Forgetting small-angle condition
  • Using SHM formulas outside validity
