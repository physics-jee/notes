Kinetic Theory of Gases

Microscopic origin of pressure, temperature, and internal energy.

1. Molecular Model of an Ideal Gas

The kinetic theory models a gas as a large number of identical molecules moving randomly in all directions.

  • Molecules are point masses
  • Intermolecular forces negligible (except during collisions)
  • Collisions are perfectly elastic
KTG replaces macroscopic averages with microscopic statistics.

2. Origin of Gas Pressure

Gas pressure arises due to repeated collisions of molecules with container walls.

\[ P = \frac{1}{3}\rho \overline{c^2} \]
  • \(\rho\): mass density of gas
  • \(\overline{c^2}\): mean square speed
Pressure is a momentum-transfer phenomenon.

3. Molecular Speeds

Gas molecules do not all move with the same speed. We describe them using averages.

Most probable speed: \[ c_p = \sqrt{\frac{2kT}{m}} \]
Mean speed: \[ \bar c = \sqrt{\frac{8kT}{\pi m}} \]
RMS speed: \[ c_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\frac{3kT}{m}} \]
Temperature controls speed distribution, not individual molecular motion.

4. Temperature and Kinetic Energy

Absolute temperature is a measure of average translational kinetic energy.

\[ \frac{1}{2}m\overline{c^2} = \frac{3}{2}kT \]
Temperature is a statistical quantity, not a property of a single molecule.

5. Ideal Gas Equation from KTG

Using molecular interpretation, the ideal gas equation can be derived.

\[ PV = NkT \]

Where \(N\) is number of molecules and \(k\) is Boltzmann constant.

Gas laws emerge naturally from molecular motion.

6. Internal Energy of an Ideal Gas

Internal energy equals total kinetic energy of all molecules.

\[ U = \frac{f}{2}NkT \]
  • \(f\): degrees of freedom
  • Monatomic gas: \(f = 3\)
Ideal gas has no potential energy contribution.

7. Degrees of Freedom

Monatomic
  • 3 translational
  • \(U = \frac{3}{2}RT\)
Diatomic (room temp)
  • 3 translational + 2 rotational
  • \(U = \frac{5}{2}RT\)
Degrees of freedom depend on temperature.

8. Specific Heats from KTG

\[ C_V = \frac{f}{2}R \quad,\quad C_P = C_V + R \]
\[ \gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V} \]
Heat capacities reflect energy storage modes.

9. Limitations of KTG

  • Fails at high pressure
  • Fails at low temperature
  • Cannot explain liquefaction
Real gas behavior requires intermolecular forces.

10. Common Mistakes

  • Interpreting temperature of a single molecule
  • Mixing macroscopic and microscopic constants
  • Forgetting factor of 3 in RMS speed
