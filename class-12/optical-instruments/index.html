Optical Instruments

Conceptual Overview

Optical instruments use mirrors, lenses, and prisms to enhance vision or capture images. This chapter covers the working of the simple microscope, compound microscope, and telescopes.

Core Formulas

\[ m = 1 + \frac{D}{f}, \quad M = -\frac{v_o}{u_o} \left( 1 + \frac{D}{f_e} \right), \quad M = \frac{f_o}{f_e} \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Visual angle and Angular magnification.
  • Simple microscope: Magnifying power for distinct vision and infinity.
  • Compound microscope: Magnification of objective and eyepiece.
  • Telescopes: Refracting (Astronomical, Terrestrial) and Reflecting (Cassegrain).
  • Resolving power of microscopes and telescopes (Rayleigh criterion).
