Alternating Current (AC)

Conceptual Overview

Alternating current (AC) is electric current that periodically reverses its direction and changes its magnitude continuously with time. It is the dominant form of power distribution.

Core Formulas

\[ V = V_0 \sin(\omega t), \quad V_{rms} = \frac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}}, \quad Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Phasors and phase relationships in R, L, and C circuits.
  • Impedance ($Z$) and Reactance ($X_L, X_C$).
  • Series LCR circuit and the phenomenon of resonance ($\omega_0 = 1/\sqrt{LC}$).
  • Power in AC circuits: Real power, Apparent power, and Power Factor.
  • Transformers: Principle of operation and energy losses.
