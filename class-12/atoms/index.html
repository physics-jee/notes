Atoms

Conceptual Overview

Explores the structure of the atom, focusing on Rutherford's alpha-scattering experiment and Bohr's quantized model of the hydrogen atom.

Core Formulas

\[ E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2} \text{ eV}, \quad \frac{1}{\lambda} = RZ^2 \left(\frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2}\right) \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Rutherford's conclusions about the nucleus.
  • Bohr's postulates: Energy levels, orbits, and angular momentum quantization.
  • Energy transitions and emission/absorption spectra (Lyman, Balmer, etc.).
  • Successes and limitations of the Bohr model.
