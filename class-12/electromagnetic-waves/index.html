Electromagnetic Waves

Conceptual Overview

Electromagnetic waves are coupled electric and magnetic fields oscillating perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation. They do not require a material medium.

Core Formulas

\[ c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}, \quad \frac{E_0}{B_0} = c, \quad I = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_0 E_0^2 c \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Displacement current: Modifying Ampere's Law for time-varying electric fields.
  • Speed of light in vacuum and its relation to permittivity and permeability.
  • Energy and Momentum carried by EM waves; Radiation Pressure.
  • EM Spectrum: Categorization from Radio waves to Gamma rays by frequency/wavelength.
