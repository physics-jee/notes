Geometrical Optics (Ray Optics)

Conceptual Overview

Ray optics describes light propagation in terms of rays. It covers reflection and refraction at plane and spherical surfaces, forming the basis of optical systems.

Core Formulas

\[ \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}, \quad n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2, \quad \frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right) \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Mirror formula and Lens maker's formula; sign conventions.
  • Laws of Refraction (Snell's Law) and Total Internal Reflection (TIR).
  • Refraction through a prism: Angle of minimum deviation and dispersion.
  • Optical instruments: Simple and Compound microscopes, Telescopes.
