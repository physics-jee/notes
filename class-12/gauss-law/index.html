Gauss's Law

Conceptual Overview

Gauss's law relates the net electric flux through a closed surface (Gaussian surface) to the net charge enclosed within that surface. It is fundamental for solving high-symmetry problems.

Core Formulas

\[ \Phi_E = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{encl}}{\epsilon_0} \]

Key Discussion Points

  • Electric flux through a closed surface is independent of shape/size.
  • Applications: Electric field due to infinite wire, plane sheet, and spherical shell.
  • Properties of conductors: Field is zero inside, net charge resides on the surface.
